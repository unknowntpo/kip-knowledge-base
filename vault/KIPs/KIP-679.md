## Summary

Turns on producer idempotence by default (enable.idempotence=true, acks=all) so out-of-the-box producers get exactly-once-in-order delivery per partition.

## Motivation

Idempotence existed since 0.11 but was off by default, so most producers silently ran at-least-once and could reorder or duplicate records on retry.

Safe defaults matter: the majority of users never tune these configs, and they deserve the strongest guarantee Kafka can give without extra work.

## Proposed Changes / Design

Default enable.idempotence to true, which implies acks=all and a bounded max.in.flight.requests.per.connection, preserving ordering under retries.

If a user sets configs that conflict with idempotence (e.g. acks=1), the producer either overrides sensibly or fails fast with a clear message rather than silently degrading.

## Trade-offs

> [!success]+ Benefits
> - No duplicates or reordering on retry, for free, for most users.
> - Aligns the default behavior with what users generally expect.
> - Negligible throughput cost on modern brokers.

> [!warning]+ Costs / Risks
> - acks=all can slightly raise latency versus acks=1.
> - Existing configs that assumed the old defaults may need review.
> - Edge-case config conflicts must be handled gracefully.

## Rejected Alternatives

**Keep idempotence opt-in** — Leaving it off means most producers never benefit; safe-by-default is the whole point of the change.

**Enable idempotence but keep acks=1** — acks=1 can't guarantee no data loss on leader failover, undermining the strongest-guarantee goal.
