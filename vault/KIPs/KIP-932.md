## Summary

Adds share groups — a queue-like consumption model where many consumers cooperatively read a topic without being bound to partition ownership.

## Motivation

Classic consumer groups tie parallelism to partition count: you can't have more active consumers than partitions, which is awkward for work-queue-style workloads.

Many teams want point-to-point queue semantics — competing consumers, per-message acknowledgement, redelivery — which Kafka's partition-ownership model doesn't naturally provide.

## Proposed Changes / Design

A share group lets multiple consumers read from the same partitions cooperatively. Records are delivered to individual consumers and individually acknowledged, rather than being tied to a committed offset per partition.

The broker tracks per-record delivery state (acquired, acknowledged, released) so unacknowledged records can be redelivered, enabling competing-consumer queue behavior on top of the existing log.

## Trade-offs

> [!success]+ Benefits
> - Consumer parallelism decoupled from partition count.
> - Per-message acknowledgement and redelivery — true queue semantics.
> - Reuses the existing durable log rather than a separate queue system.

> [!warning]+ Costs / Risks
> - Broker must track per-record delivery state — new state and overhead.
> - Weaker ordering guarantees than partition-ordered consumption.
> - Still maturing: shipped as early access, semantics may evolve.

## Rejected Alternatives

**A separate queue product** — Building queues as a distinct system would fragment Kafka's storage and tooling; layering share groups on the existing log reuses durability and ops.

**More partitions for parallelism** — Scaling partitions to add consumers is expensive and doesn't give per-message ack or redelivery semantics.
