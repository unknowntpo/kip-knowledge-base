## Summary

Uses a monotonically increasing leader epoch to decide log truncation on recovery, fixing data-loss and divergence edge cases in the old high-watermark scheme.

## Motivation

The old truncation logic relied only on the high watermark. After certain leader-change and crash sequences, a returning replica could truncate to the wrong offset, causing silent log divergence or data loss.

The high watermark alone can't distinguish records from different leadership terms, so a follower had no reliable way to know exactly where its log diverged from the leader's.

## Proposed Changes / Design

Each leader stamps its records with a leader epoch and maintains a leader-epoch-to-start-offset map. On becoming follower, a replica asks the leader for the end offset of its last known epoch.

The follower truncates precisely to that offset — the true point of divergence — instead of guessing from the high watermark, guaranteeing logs converge correctly.

## Trade-offs

> [!success]+ Benefits
> - Eliminates a class of silent data-loss and divergence bugs.
> - Truncation is precise and provably correct across leader changes.
> - Foundation for later correctness work in the replication layer.

> [!warning]+ Costs / Risks
> - Adds a leader-epoch cache and a new inter-broker request.
> - On-disk and protocol format changes require careful upgrade handling.

## Rejected Alternatives

**Tune high-watermark timing** — No amount of timing tweaks fixes the fundamental inability of the high watermark to identify the divergence point across epochs.
