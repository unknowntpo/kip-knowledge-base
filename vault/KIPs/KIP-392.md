## Summary

Lets consumers read from a nearby follower instead of always the leader, cutting cross-datacenter and cross-AZ network cost and latency.

## Motivation

Consumers historically had to fetch only from the partition leader. In multi-AZ or multi-region deployments that meant expensive, high-latency cross-zone reads even when a local replica held the data.

Cross-AZ traffic is a major cloud cost line item; forcing all reads through the leader inflates both bill and tail latency.

## Proposed Changes / Design

Brokers expose rack/zone metadata and a pluggable replica selector. The consumer sends its own rack id; the broker replies with the preferred read replica for that location.

Followers serve reads only up to the high watermark, preserving consumer consistency, while the leader still handles all writes.

## Trade-offs

> [!success]+ Benefits
> - Large cut in cross-AZ / cross-region network cost.
> - Lower read latency by staying within a zone.
> - Pluggable selector lets operators encode their own topology.

> [!warning]+ Costs / Risks
> - Follower reads can lag the leader up to the high watermark.
> - Requires accurate rack/zone configuration to pay off.
> - Adds replica-selection logic to the fetch path.

## Rejected Alternatives

**Client-side leader avoidance** — Without broker-provided topology and high-watermark guarantees, clients can't safely choose a replica without risking inconsistent reads.
